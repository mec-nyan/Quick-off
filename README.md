# Quick off

Quick GNOME Shell exit dialogue for busy people.

# What? Why?

Don't worry my dear friend, there will be answers for all your questions (at least for those two).

**What:** This is a very simple GNOME Shell extension that gives you a "quick" exit dialogue with the expected options (power off, restart, log out, lock screen). Of course, you can choose what options will be presented to you in the extension preferences.

**Why:** There's a short answer and a long one (of course)...

### The short answer

I think that for most people, turning your computer off should be at two key presses (or clicks) away, maximum.

- One key press to tell your computer to power off (or restart, log out, etc.).
- A second one to confirm (just in case you gave the command by mistake ha ha).

That's what this extension is all about (and just that).

- Select the default option (and extra options) in the preferences.
- Hit the key-binding once (or click the icon).
- Hit enter to confirm, or escape to cancel.

That's it.

Of course, you can add other exit options (restart, log out, lock screen) and use Tab to choose when you don't want the default action.
